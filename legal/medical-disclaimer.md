## Part 1 — In Plain Language

**Chaos Command is a tool for keeping track of your own health information. It is not a
doctor, and it does not give medical advice.**

- The app **helps you record and organize** symptoms, medications, appointments, and
  patterns — so *you* can see your own trends and bring better information to the people
  who actually treat you.
- It does **not diagnose anything, treat anything, or tell you what to do** about your
  health. Charts and patterns it shows are summaries of what *you* entered, not medical
  conclusions.
- **Never use this app to decide whether to take, change, or stop a medication or
  treatment on your own.** Talk to a qualified healthcare professional. If you think
  you're having an emergency, call your local emergency number (in the US, 911) or go
  to an emergency room — do not rely on this app.
- The app is a **record-keeper, not a monitor.** It does not watch over you, alert
  anyone, or call for help. Reminders only appear when you open the app.
- **You are responsible for your own data and backups.** Keep your own backups (the app
  makes this easy and encrypted). We have no copy of your data and cannot recover it if
  your device is lost or wiped.

We built this because tracking your own health *should* be in your hands. Keeping it in
your hands also means the medical decisions stay between you and your care team — where
they belong.

## Part 2 — The Formal Version

### 1. Not Medical Advice
The Chaos Command application ("App") is an informational and personal record-keeping
tool. The App, its content, and any output, summary, chart, pattern, or notification it
generates do not constitute medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment, and are not a
substitute for professional medical judgment. Nothing in the App establishes a
physician–patient or other care-provider relationship.

### 2. No Diagnosis or Treatment Function
The App does not diagnose, cure, mitigate, treat, or prevent any disease or condition.
Any trends, correlations, or visualizations presented by the App are arithmetic
summaries of user-entered data and must not be interpreted as clinical findings.

### 3. Consult Qualified Professionals
Users should always seek the advice of a physician or other qualified health provider
with any questions regarding a medical condition, medication, or treatment, and should
never disregard, delay, or alter professional medical advice or treatment because of
information recorded in or generated by the App.

### 4. Emergencies
The App is not an emergency service and does not perform monitoring, detection, or
alerting. It will not contact emergency services, caregivers, or any third party. In a
medical emergency, users must contact their local emergency services (e.g., 911 in the
United States) or seek immediate in-person care.

### 5. Not a Medical Device
The App is not intended to be, and has not been evaluated or cleared by the U.S. Food
and Drug Administration or any comparable regulatory authority as, a medical device. It
is intended solely for general informational and personal organizational use.

### 6. Accuracy and Reliance
The App reflects only the data a user chooses to enter and does not verify the accuracy
or completeness of that data. Reminders, schedules, and computed values may be affected
by user input, device state, or software conditions, and must not be solely relied upon
for time- or safety-critical health decisions (including medication timing).

### 7. User Responsibility for Data and Backups
All data resides on the user's device. The Company maintains no copy and cannot recover
data lost through device loss, damage, reset, deletion, or software issues. The user is
solely responsible for maintaining backups, for safeguarding any exported file and its
passphrase, and for the security of the device on which data is stored.
